Research Participant Recruitment
Control Performance Evaluation of Tactile Presentation by Tabletop Mobile Robot

한국어 日本語 English

Study Content

This study conducts an additional experiment to evaluate the control performance of "stroking touch" presented on the forearm by a tabletop mobile robot. The robot gently strokes the arm, and we measure physical parameters (force, speed, straightness, etc.) along with subjective evaluations of the tactile sensation.

Tactile presentation experiment with tabletop mobile robot

ℹ️ Feel free to participate

This experiment involves no pain stimulation whatsoever. The presented tactile sensation is a gentle touch similar to having your forearm softly stroked by a partner (approx. 0.4N ≒ approx. 40g).

  • Only mild tactile stimulation at everyday-life level
  • Virtually no risk of health damage or invasiveness
  • You may freely pause or stop the experiment at any time

Eligibility Criteria and Exclusions

Target Participants: Healthy adults aged 18 or older (University of Tsukuba students and nearby residents)

❌ Exclusion Criteria:

  • Those with visual or auditory difficulties that would impair recognition of visual stimuli or comprehension of verbal instructions during the experiment
  • Those unable to communicate sufficiently in Japanese
  • Those with central brain disorders or psychiatric conditions (moderate or higher severity currently under treatment/medication as diagnosed by a physician)
  • Those with wounds or burn marks at the stimulation/sensor attachment site (forearm)
  • Those who have suffered burns at the stimulation/sensor attachment site within the past 6 months
  • Those with chronic neuralgia
  • Those using a cardiac pacemaker

🛡️ Safety Considerations

This experiment does not use any pain stimulation. The presented tactile sensation is a mild stimulus at everyday-life level (approx. 0.4N), with virtually no risk of health damage.

Participant Rights • Right to pause or stop the experiment at any time
• Right to freely remove the stimulated body part from the device
• Right to receive compensation based on time spent even if discontinued
Safety Design • Maximum pressing force of the stroking module is approx. 1.9N (approx. 200g) — no greater force can be applied
• Barricade installed between the robot and participant's arm to prevent direct contact
• Emergency stop switches installed on both participant and experimenter sides
Insurance Coverage In the unlikely event of injury or harm from this experiment, compensation will be provided through the National University Association Insurance.
